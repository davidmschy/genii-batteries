Frequently Asked Questions

What is the IRS Section 48C tax credit?
Section 48C provides a federal tax credit of $35 per kWh for qualifying energy storage systems. For the G3000 (3,000 kWh), that's $105,000 to $150,000 depending on your installation configuration. This is a direct credit against your federal tax liability.
How safe is saltwater chemistry compared to lithium?
Saltwater flow batteries use aqueous (water-based) electrolyte that cannot catch fire or explode. Unlike lithium-ion systems, there's no thermal runaway risk, no toxic gas release, and no need for expensive fire suppression systems. Your insurance premiums reflect this safety advantage.
What does "self-healing electrodes" mean?
Unlike lithium batteries that degrade with every charge cycle, saltwater flow batteries use liquid electrolyte that can be refreshed or replaced. The electrodes regenerate during operation, maintaining performance for 25+ years without significant capacity loss.
Can I scale beyond 3 MWh?
Yes. The G3000 is modular and can be paralleled with additional units to reach 6 MWh, 9 MWh, or higher. Each unit operates independently, so you can start with one and expand as your energy needs grow.
What's included in the installation?
Full turnkey installation includes: G3000 system delivery, foundation/pad preparation, electrical interconnection, grid synchronization, permitting support, utility approvals, remote monitoring setup, and operator training. You receive a fully commissioned, ready-to-operate system.
Why is delivery so much faster than Tesla?
We manufacture on-demand with lean inventory and shorter supply chains. Tesla's Megapack has an 18+ month backlog due to overwhelming demand. The G3000 uses simpler, more abundant materials (saltwater vs lithium), allowing us to scale production quickly without rare earth constraints.
